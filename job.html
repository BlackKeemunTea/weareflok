The opportunity

Whitley Fund for Nature is a UK-based charity backing exceptional wildlife conservationists across the Global South. As Head of Communications, you'll lead the organisation's entire external voice — from press and media strategy to digital channels, storytelling, and fundraising communications.

This is a senior leadership role for someone who knows how to build and protect a mission-led brand, and who can translate complex conservation work into compelling narratives for donors, media, and the public.

What you'll do
  • Lead and develop the communications function — currently a small, high-performing team of three
  • Own the editorial strategy across all owned, earned, and paid channels
  • Manage media relations and act as a senior spokesperson where required
  • Partner closely with the fundraising team to support major donor and trust communications
  • Oversee digital presence including website, social, and email
  • Represent WFN at sector events and within the conservation communications community
What we're looking for
  • Significant senior communications experience, ideally within the charity, NGO, or nature/environment sector
  • Proven track record of building or significantly developing a communications function
  • Excellent written and verbal communication — strong editor and confident presenter
  • Experience working closely with senior leadership and boards
  • Genuine commitment to conservation, sustainability, or the natural world
About Whitley Fund for Nature

Founded in 1993, Whitley Fund for Nature has awarded over £20 million to more than 200 conservationists in 80+ countries. The charity funds and champions grassroots leaders doing exceptional work protecting wildlife and wild places.

WFN is a small, ambitious team based in London, operating with the energy and agility of a startup and the credibility of a 30-year institution.

Working here
  • Fully remote with optional co-working space in London
  • Quarterly in-person team days
  • Annual staff retreat
  • Flexible hours within a core 10am–4pm window
  • Genuine work-life balance — this is not a culture of overwork
